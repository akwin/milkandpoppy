The reasons I cook

Eating is a necessity but cooking is an art.

I didn't realize the importance or necessity of cooking until i moved away from India. I could depend on many external factors to obtain well-prepared delicious food - from mom's delicious meals to the many wonders of indian street foods and in recent years, take out and home delivery. However, moving to Vancouver brought about a big shift in terms of availability of foods that i liked and was used to. I could only eat takeout for so long. And so my journey into the vast vast world of cooking began. Here are a few things i have learnt until now. This list is by no means complete as you're always learning in the kitchen. And out of it.

  1. It's a life-saving skill to acquire.
  2. The investment : returns ratio tops the charts. Spend a few minutes reading a recipe and playing with good looking ingredients in the kitchen and reward yourself with a five star dinner.
  3. With some effort, a bit of patience and practice, your meals will taste better than ever before. Mostly because you will learn to cook according to your taste and you'll compare your own creations with all other dishes.
  4. Cooking gives you the opportunity to orchestrate something amazing - you take some ingredients, chop them, mix them, cook them a certain way and magic happens! You are a creator - you've made that delicious meal & you deserve all of it.
  5. It helps you earn brownie points with every single relationship in your life. Cook with and for your family and friends. It's a joy to see their wonder, exhilaration and satisfaction after successfully impressing them with a good dish.
  6. Sometimes, a warm bowl of delicious soup is all you need and being able to fulfill a need exactly to perfection is a rare opportunity. And cooking can help you reach this accomplishment.
  7. Cooking has never been a chore for me and shouldn't be for you too. It is a quiet time to be one with your food, be in-charge of what you are eating and as a consequence on the path to eating healthy and well. For me cooking is a fun way to wind down. It's exciting to pick new items in the grocery store and transform them into something delicious.
  8. You eventually develop your own personal stash of recipes.
So, whether you're a beginner or an expert, you're definitely someone who eats food, multiple times a day. Food is what makes us and keeps our bodies running and it would be worthwhile to be conscious of your dietary habits. And venture into the kitchen once in a while and treat yourself to a date. Also, share with me your reasons to cook!
