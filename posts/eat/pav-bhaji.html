Pav Bhaji

If there's one thing every indian living abroad misses, it's the epic food. And street food most of all. In the past few years of living and cooking of my own, i have been experimenting in the kitchen with my own versions of some all time favorites. This is my version of the very deservingly popular and tasty dish known as Pav Bhaji - literally translated to veggie mash with buns.

For four servings, You will need:
  • Potatoes, four
  • Carrots, two
  • Peas
  • Cauliflower, halved
  • Beans
  • Literally any vegetable you fancy

    • For tempering

  • Onions, two
  • Tomatoes, three
  • Ginger
  • Green chillies
  • Jeera or Cumin powder
  • Dhaniya powder or Coriander power
  • Red chilli powder
  • Turmeric
  • MDH Pav Bhaji Masala
  • Kasoori Methi
  • Dry mango powder or amchur
  • Salt
  • Cilantro leaves
  • Lemon

Instructions

Chop the veggies (except onions and tomatoes) and put them in a pressure cooker.

Cook the vegetables for four-five whistles.

Tip: if you're in a hurry and cannot wait for the steam to release with time, hold your pressure cooker under running cold water and the steam will release in a few seconds.

If you do not have a pressure cooker, use the utensil you use to make soups and boil the vegetables till they are mushy.

Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds when hot.

Add thinely sliced ginger and green chillies.

Chop the onions very thinly and cook till they turn pink and the bitter smell goes away.

Puree the tomatoes (you can substitute with tomato paste) and cook with the onions.

Cover the pan and let the masala cook undisturbed for five minutes.

The mixture will release oil on the sides and this is an apt time to add the spices.

Sprinkle coriander powder, red chilli powder, turmeric and salt. Mix well.

It's time to add the cooked vegetables and let them absorb the flavors of the masala.

Mash the vegetables using a potato masher or your plain old ladle.

Mix in the pav bhaji masala and sprinkle kasoori methi on top.

Garnish with a generous squeeze of lemon, chopped onion and fresh coriander leaves.

On a skillet, melt some butter.

Cut the buns or pav in half and toast them.

Enjoy!
